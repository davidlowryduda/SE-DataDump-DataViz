## Body:
<p>In a <a href="http://math.stackexchange.com/questions/183259/collatz-ish-olympiad-problem">recent question</a> the following recursive sequence was considered:
$$
    a_{n+1} = \cases{\frac{a_n}{2} &amp; $a_n$ is even \\ a_n +d &amp; $a_n$ is odd}, \quad a_1 = d + 1
$$
where $d$ is an odd positive integer. Such a sequence will eventually reach $1$, and let $m(d)$ be the value of the index, where the sequence element equals one, $a_m = 1$. Here is a visualization of $m(d)$:
  <img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/j4qAP.png" alt="Plot of the length of Collatz-like sequence as a function of parameter d"></p>

<p>It is apparent from the plot that $m(d) \leqslant 3 \left\lfloor \frac{d-1}{2} \right\rfloor$. Selecting those $d$ which saturate the bound:
<img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/8rBoG.png" alt="enter image description here">
OEIS tells us that this is sequence <a href="http://oeis.org/A001122">A001122</a> of "<em>primes with primitive root</em> $2$". </p>

<p>This begs for an explanation. References, intuitive insights, solutions are welcome.</p>
