<p>When the prime is a reasonably small one I'd rather find directly the inverse:
$$9^{-1}=\frac{1}{9}=3\pmod {13}\Longrightarrow 9x=7\Longrightarrow x=7\cdot 9^{-1}=7\cdot 3= 21=8\pmod {13}$$
<strong>But</strong>...I try Gauss's method when the prime is big and/or evaluating inverses is messy.</p>
