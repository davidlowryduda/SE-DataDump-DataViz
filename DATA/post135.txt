## Title:
Applications of Bounds of the Sum of Inverse Prime Divisors $\sum_{p \mid n} \frac{1}{p}$

## Tags:
<number-theory><reference-request>

## Score:
2

## Body:
<p><strong>Question</strong>: What interesting or notable problems involve the additive function $\sum_{p \mid n} \frac{1}{p}$ and depend on sharp bounds of this function? </p>

<p>I'm aware of at least one: A certain upper bound of the sum implies the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abc_conjecture" rel="nofollow">ABC conjecture</a>.</p>

<p>By the Arithmetic-Geometric Inequality, one can bound the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radical_of_an_integer" rel="nofollow">radical function</a> (squarefree kernel) of an integer,
\begin{align}
\left( \frac{\omega(n)}{\sum_{p \mid n} \frac{1}{p}} \right)^{\omega(n)} \leqslant \text{rad}(n).
\end{align}
If for any $\epsilon &gt; 0$ there exists a finite constant $K_{\epsilon}$ such that for any triple $(a,b,c)$ of coprime positive integers, where $c = a + b$, one has
\begin{align}
\sum_{p \mid abc} \frac{1}{p} &lt; \omega(abc) \left( \frac{K_\epsilon}{c} \right)^{1/((1+\epsilon) \omega(abc))},
\end{align}
then
\begin{align}
c &lt; K_{\epsilon} \left( \frac{\omega(abc)}{\sum_{p \mid abc} \frac{1}{p}} \right)^{(1+ \epsilon)\omega(abc)} \leqslant \text{rad}(abc)^{1+\epsilon},
\end{align}
and the ABC-conjecture is true.</p>

<p><em>Edit:</em> Now, whether or not any triples satisfy the bound on inverse primes is a separate issue. Greg Martin points out that there are infinitely many triples which indeed violate it. This begs the question of whether there are any further refinements of the arithmetic-geometric inequality which remove such anomalies, but this question is secondary.</p>
