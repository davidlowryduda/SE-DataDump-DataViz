## Body:
<p>For what it's worth, Dickson, in his <em>History of the Theory of Numbers</em>, Chapter VII, page $186$, credits Jacobi (<em>Canon Arithmeticus</em>, $1839$) with the result that if $p$ is an odd prime, a primitive root of $p^2$ is a primitive root of $p^k$ for all $k&gt; 2$.   </p>
