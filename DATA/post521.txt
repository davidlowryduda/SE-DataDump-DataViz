<p>There are many un-serious papers on this subject at the same site, I selected the above from papers that I read or authors whose other papers I have seen.</p>

<p>There is also a celebrated theorem by John H Conway in which he shows that the natural generalization of the Collatz problem, where different linear functions are applied to $n$ in several arithmetic progressions (partitioning all integers), <strong>can simulate a computer</strong>.  A consequence is that it is undecidable to determine if such an iteration loops when started from $1$.</p>
